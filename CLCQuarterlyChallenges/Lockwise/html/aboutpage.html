About Lockwise

How did it Start?

Lockwise, a project initiated by Haya Raham Asif, found its roots during a quarterly challenge within the TAP Program as part of CLC. With a genuine interest in cybersecurity, the creation of Lockwise wasn't about grand ambitions but rather a humble effort to contribute to the field. This blog-type site aims to provide helpful posts on essential topics related to cybersecurity, an attempt to share insights and knowledge. In the spirit of continuous learning fostered by the TAP program, Lockwise hopes to make a meaningful impact within the cybersecurity community.

About the Creator

Meet Haya Raham Asif, a ninth-grade student with a knack for Python and a talent for building websites using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Beyond the world of coding, she enjoys reading a good book and savoring tasty meals. Haya is currently working on starting a coding workshop for young kids, introducing them to the basics of programming through platforms like Scratch. Her passion lies not just in coding for herself but in sharing the excitement of technology with the next generation.

Canada Learning Code

Canada Learning Code believes that digital skills are tools of empowerment. The world is changing and they want Canada to be ready. They're here to make sure that all people in Canada, - particularly women transgender and beyond binary individuals racialized people - have access to the knowledge they need to prosper in a digital world.
